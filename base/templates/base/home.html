Premier League Sentimeter

Sentiment Analysis

This site uses twitter data to gauge the overall sentiment about a soccer team and its players. Using natural language processing, a sample of tweets about a particular subject are analyzed. For example, the following tweet is classified as having a positive sentiment (+0.70):

Wayne Rooney: a great player who gave everything for the England and the Man United shirt

— isaac (@_isaacmx) July 9, 2017

While this tweet is classified as having a negative sentiment (-0.60):

Surprised to see such positivity around Rooney's Everton move in my TL. Want him to do well but it's obviously a bad signing for Everton.

— Tiago Estêvão (@TiagoEstv) July 9, 2017

Team Sentiment

For each team, its sentiment score history is displayed in a line graph. These scores are determined by averaging the sentiment score of a sample of up to 1000 tweets containing the team's name over the past day. Currently, the database contains only data for Premier League teams.

Tweet Location

Locations of tweets about a particular team are displayed via twitter geolocation data. Note that this data only exists for a small fraction of tweets.

Player Sentiment

Player scores are determined the same way as those of teams. means a positive change from the previous day, means a negative change, and means the score was unchanged or no tweets were found. Note that small sample size may skew some player scores. Hovering over each player gives a small sample of text from Wikipedia describing the player. Note that recently transferred players may not appear in the database.

Navigation

Links to each team's page can be found in the sidebar menu. The compare tool allows for the data of two teams to be displayed simultaneously and compared.
